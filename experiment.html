1 Experiment

1.1 Data collection

Data was collected using a internet-based survey form, which has been programmed with the CodeIgniter framework. Demographic data involved gender, age and nationality. To be able to recognise double respondents, the IP address was registered, as well as the date and start/end time. Lastly, the admission mode (email, another website) was collected.

1.2 Stimulus persons

Stimulus persons were created by merging two faces together, the base (normal) pictures are used by courtesy of Prof. Martin Gründl from Beautycheck. The faces were then modified having exophoria (why?), acne, eyebrow piercing or temporal tattoo with Adobe Photoshop.

1.3 Survey structure

Participants were allocated first to either getting an appendectomy or having a medical checkup (variable situation) with a probability of 0.5 each. They were subsequently allocated to one of five treatment groups (variable trt_group) being 0 the control group with a probability of 0.2 each. The participants were shown four pictures according to the following order:

Picture order by treatment group
Treatment group
0 (Control) A0 B0 C0 D0
1 A1 B2 C3 D4
2 A2 B3 C4 D1
3 A3 B4 C1 D2
4 A4 B1 C2 D3

For every picture, participants were asked, how likely they would have the operation or checkup performed by this physician on a Likert scale from 0 (not at all) to 10 (definitely) (variable answer). They also had to rate the shown physician regarding their perceived reliability, honesty, attractiveness, intelligence, compentence and kindness, again on a Likert scale from 0 (not at all) to 10 (maximal).
